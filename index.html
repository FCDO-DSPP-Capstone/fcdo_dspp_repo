Data Science Analysis for the International Tech Department of the FCDO

1. United Nations votes in the General Assembly

This plot is made using all the votes in the General Assembly of the United Nations for a given year (you can select any between 1990 and 2024). Each point is a country. Countries that are closer vote together more consistently than countries that are far apart.
Click in the continents names in the legend to hide/show them in the graph. The GDP PP and Population sliders act as filters to limit the number of countries displayed at once. They are represented as percentile values (e.g population in range 50% to 100% is the 50% more populated countries). You can zoom in and out scrolling. Double click to reset view.

* Data sources:
- UN GA vote records in: https://digitallibrary.un.org/record/4060887/
- World Bank Data: https://data.worldbank.org

2. United Nations General Opening Speeches

This plots are made using the UN General Debate speeches from 1992 to 2023. Each country gives one speech per year.
For this plots, each speech was split into many sentences. Then, each sentence is classified into a topic using Natural Language Processing techniques. Finally, we selected only those sentenced classified in a technological related topics.

The first plot in this section is a network graph with colored nodes representing countries and gray nodes representing topics. The width of the edges is proportional to the number of times a topic was mentioned by a country, with edges colored according to the countryâ€™s color. Clicking on a node will render a second plot displaying the selected node and its connections.
You need to select a sub-set of countries from the dropdown menu. The US, the UK and China are always in the plot.
You can zoom in the plot and double click to reset view.

* Data sources:
- United Nations General Debate Corpus 1946-2023 at: https://dataverse.harvard.edu/dataset.xhtml?persistentId=doi:10.7910/DVN/0TJX8Y

3. Network of Technology Research

This plot visualizes international scientific collaborations as a network, with each node representing a country. Connections indicate collaboration between countries.
Select a research topic, a time range, and a country to highlight. The selected country will appear in green, its collaborators in red, and non-connected countries in blue. This makes it easy to identify main collaborators and potential areas for new partnerships.

Node size is proportional to the number of collaborations. Each collaboration involves at least two authors affiliated with a Research institution. Collaborations are attributed to the country of the institution, not the author's nationality. For example, a collaboration between the US and China means one author is affiliated with a US institution and a co-author with a Chinese institution.
